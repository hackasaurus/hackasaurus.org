<h1>All about Narwhals</h1> <p>Narwhals are whales that live in the Arctic. They have a single helical tusk and swim at depths of up to 1500m.</p> <h2>The Narwhal's tusk</h2> <p>It is believed the purpose of the Narwhal's tusk is to determine social rank. Narwhals have rarely been seen using their tusk for fighting or breaking sea ice.</p> <h2>Population</h2> <p>There are estimated to be around 75,000 Narwhals in the wild, and they are a near threatened species.</p> <h3>Conservation</h3> <p>Narwhals are one of the most vulnerable arctic marine animals to climate change.</p> <h4>Threats</h4> <p>The only predators to Narwhals besides humans are polar bears and killar whales.</p> <h2>Narwhal diet</h2> <p>The diet of the Narwhal is very restricted. They prey on halibut, cod, shrimp and squid.</p> <h3>Narwhal feeding habits</h3> <p>Narwhals feed in deep water, and congregate in groups of five to ten. <h4>Diving</h4> <p>Narwhals are excellent divers and make some of the deepest dives ever recorded for marine mammals.</p>
